Wireframe Kit · Low fidelity · Project-agnostic

Breadboard vocabulary for client-app wireframes

Grey breadboard building blocks for wireframing any client app — mobile or web/desktop. A component library, two device canvases (a phone frame and a browser window), screen archetypes for both, and an options-comparison layout. Regions, hierarchy, and flow only; no brand color or final type. Delete the parts you are not presenting, fill in your screen(s), and publish or export the result. See the wireframe-design skill.

Wireframe component library

The common UI components client apps share (the shadcn-UI / Material vocabulary), at breadboard fidelity (regions, hierarchy, flow — no color or final type). Each shows its common states and variant axes for clarity — states (default, filled, focus, invalid, selected, disabled), and where they apply importance (primary/secondary/tertiary), tone (neutral/accent/destructive), size (sm/md/lg), and loading.

Breadboard, wireflow & annotation

Flow-level artifacts for when the open question is topology, sequence, or intent rather than one screen's regions. Breadboard a flow in words before drawing it; join screens into a wireflow with arrows rooted on the control that drives each transition (solid = primary path, dashed = alternative / edge path); and carry intent in an annotation layer whose color (--anno) is reserved so a note is never read as an active element or an error.

Options-comparison layout

An options round presents at least three distinct candidates side by side, in whichever canvas the project uses (mobile or web). Give each a label, a sketch, a rationale, and its trade-offs; mark exactly one (Recommended). Screens shared across all options render once above the grid (omitted here).
